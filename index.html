INDIAN RAILWAYS

Overview

Indian Railways, established in 1853, is the fourth-largest railway network in the world, managed by the Government of India under the Ministry of Railways. It is not just a mode of transport but a lifeline connecting the nation's cities, towns, and villages. With over 67,000 km of track and 23 million daily passengers, it plays a crucial role in Indiaâ€™s socio-economic fabric.

Government



The Ministry of Railways oversees Indian Railways, with its operations divided into 18 railway zones, each led by a General Manager. A centralised structure ensures smooth governance, focusing on efficiency, safety, and technological advancements. The Railway Board, under the Railway Minister, formulates policies and ensures their implementation.


The first Railway Minister of independent India was John Mathai (1947-1948) , who helped shape the initial policies of Indian Railways after independence.

The current Railway Minister of India is Ashwini Vaishnaw . He has held this position since July 2021 and is responsible for overseeing the modernization and expansion of Indian Railways. Under his leadership, initiatives such as the expansion of the Vande Bharat Express, 100% electrification of routes, and the Mumbai-Ahmedabad bullet train project have gained momentum. He is also focused on digitalization, station redevelopment, and enhancing passenger safety.

It is also one of the largest employers in the country, with over 1.2 million employees working in various capacities.
